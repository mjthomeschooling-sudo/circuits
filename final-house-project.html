BRINGING IT ALL HOME: THE CAPSTONE CIRCUITS PROJECT

What This Stage Was About

This was the culminating stage of the entire circuits journey. While the physical blueprints, the 3D TinkerCAD model, and the templates were created prior to this unit, this final stage focused on bringing those plans to life. After mastering circuit concepts and prototyping features, my student finally built the official foam board structure and integrated all his electronic systems into one final, polished engineering project.

This stage mattered because it brought all the individual tools, concepts, and prior architectural planning together into one complete engineering project.

Tools We Used

TinkerCAD PICRAT: Creative–Transformation

Micro:bit PICRAT: Creative–Transformation

Used to program and fine-tune the final code for the integrated building systems.

Implemented for manual features like basic lighting, providing direct-wired alternatives to the smart features.

Capstone Summary

My capstone demonstrates my mastery of Digital Navigation by showing how I moved across multiple digital tools, platforms, and representations to guide my student from architectural planning to a fully integrated electronic foam house. The process began with him drafting physical blueprints and translating them into a 3D model in TinkerCAD, establishing the structural foundation for the build. From there, my student and I shifted into the circuits unit, using digital resources such as BBC Bitesize and the PhET Circuit Construction Kit to build conceptual understanding before applying those ideas with Snap Circuits. We then moved into the Micro:bit stage, navigating between the MakeCode editor, the micro:bit simulator, and physical hardware to learn how code controls real circuits. During the planning and prototyping phase, we created Micro:bit‑controlled versions of every feature and built standalone circuit versions only for the features that could be replicated without a micro:bit, allowing us to compare wiring, logic, and behavior before deciding what to integrate into the final house. As we entered the final stages of the capstone, we used his architectural templates to construct the foam house and then integrated the coded systems, testing and refining the interactive features.

Across the capstone, these stages demonstrate how intentional digital navigation, thoughtful scaffolding, and a carefully sequenced pathway—from digital exploration to physical systems to coded integration—supported a complex, authentic engineering build from start to finish and strengthened my ability to navigate digital tools with clarity and purpose.

ISTE Standards Addressed

1.1 Empowered Learner

The student applied their prototype knowledge to choose which electronic features to include in the house and made decisions about placement, wiring routes, and component positioning, taking ownership of how the final model functioned.

1.4 Innovative Designer

The student used blueprints, Tinkercad models, and hands‑on foam‑house construction to adapt and refine their designs, adjusting layouts, hiding components, and iterating on both structure and circuitry to create functional house features.

1.5 Computational Thinker

The student diagnosed and solved integration challenges such as wire length, polarity issues, blocked sensors, and unstable connections, using systematic troubleshooting and logical reasoning to ensure each feature worked reliably inside the house.

1.6 Creative Communicator

The student documented their design and installation process with sketches, digital models, photos, and explanations, clearly communicating how each circuit and feature evolved from prototype to final house integration.
