Capstone Summary

This project demonstrates my mastery of Digital Navigation by showing how I moved across multiple digital tools, platforms, and representations to design and build a fully functional electronic foam house. I began by exploring electricity concepts through online resources and simulations, using BBC Bitesize, Bill Nye, and the PhET Circuit Construction Kit to understand circuits, current, voltage, and troubleshooting in a digital environment. I then transferred those digital skills into physical systems by building and testing real circuits with Snap Circuits, learning how switches, LEDs, motors, and buzzers behave in the real world. As I designed and built the foam house systems, I navigated between the MakeCode editor, the micro:bit simulator, and the physical micro:bit hardware to write code, test logic, and control sensors, LEDs, buzzers, and servo motors. Throughout the process, I made decisions about wiring, power, inputs, outputs, and automation, and I solved problems when circuits or code didn’t work as expected. This capstone reflects both the planning I did and the work my son and I did together as we navigated digital tools, connected digital and physical systems, and brought the whole project to life step by step.

ISTE Standards Addressed

1.1 Empowered Learner

The student applied their prototype knowledge to choose which electronic features to install in their foam house and made decisions about placement, wiring routes, and component positioning, taking ownership of how their final model functioned.

1.4 Innovative Designer

The student adapted their breadboard prototypes to fit the physical constraints of the foam house—extending wires, hiding components, mounting sensors, and adjusting layouts—iterating on both design and installation to create functional, realistic house features.

1.5 Computational Thinker

The student diagnosed and solved real‑world integration problems such as wire length, polarity errors, blocked sensors, and unstable connections, using systematic troubleshooting and logical reasoning to ensure each feature worked reliably inside the house.

1.6 Creative Communicator

The student documented their installation process with photos, diagrams, and explanations, clearly communicating how each circuit was adapted from the prototype stage and how it functions within the final foam house model.
